# 17 — Final Editorial Decisions

Synthesis of the four post-rewrite editor reports (conciseness, clarity,
flow, intention-comparison) into a polishing playbook. The polish pass
that follows is **calibration, not structural rewrite**. The bones are
right. What remains is closure deflation, de-subsectioning where
subsections are template residue, vignette-carousel compression,
Murdoch budget enforcement, and a handful of chapter-specific structural
moves.

## Convergent diagnoses across the four reports

1. **Closure inflation.** Many chapters close twice (a coda paragraph
   that re-states the claim after the chapter has already landed). Cut
   the second closing.
2. **Subsection residue.** Several chapters use labeled subsections to
   do structural work the prose should be doing. Convert to flowing
   continuous prose where the breaks aren't earning their position
   (worst: Chs 9, 12, 17; also Chs 5, 11, 13).
3. **Carousel vignettes.** Chapter ends sometimes run 4–6 identically
   shaped concrete-situation vignettes when 2–3 would carry the work
   harder (worst: Chs 13, 14, 18).
4. **Modern-counterpoint piling.** Some chapters cycle through
   3+ modern philosophers making the same structural point. Where
   two voices say the same thing, pick the stronger and cut (Chs 7,
   13, 15, 17, 18).
5. **Commentator-rotation fatigue.** A few chapters survey 5–6
   commentators when 3 would carry the synthesis with citation
   reference for the rest (worst: Ch 11).
6. **Murdoch over-budget.** Murdoch carries 6+ chapters where the
   validated intention budgeted ≤4. Reduce her in Chs 10 and 16
   (Hopkins and Arendt are the load-bearing modern voices for
   those chapters respectively).
7. **Buried claims.** A few chapters bury the lead (worst: Ch 4 —
   4.34 should be in the lead; Ch 12 — qualities-as-consequence
   should land in the first paragraph; Ch 16 — three gates should
   surface earlier).
8. **Closing roster paragraph in Ch 7** — reads as 8-section
   residue. Cut.
