# 21 — Intention & Voice Audit of the Rewrite

This review checks whether the rewritten 18 chapters deliver what
`plan/07_voice_and_style.md`, `plan/06_book_vision.md`, and
`review/05_validated_intention.md` asked of them.

---

## Overall verdict

The rewrite delivers, mostly. The book that used to read like a
literature-review with extracted quotations now reads like a book
a busy adult could pick up at a coffee shop and use. Direct YOU
address has installed cleanly across nearly all chapters. The
8-section template is gone. Chapters lead with their claim, in
real lead-with-claim form (Ch 2's "Act fully. Do not graft your
selfhood onto the result," Ch 3's "*You cannot withdraw,*" Ch 7's
"The world is real," Ch 13's "The watcher of your weather is not
the weather," Ch 17's "Your faith is what your life does," Ch 18's
"Stop trying to save yourself by your performance"). The voice is
recognizably one author's voice across all 18. Sanskrit gets
glossed in line, mostly briefly. The four-part posture declared
in the preface — act without self-grafting; trained attention;
love-with-attention through what one has; surrender of the bargain
— is discernible across the manuscript, with each part given a
clear chapter-home. The author's eight positions hold, with small
wobbles, mostly on the bhakti accessibility/supremacy question
(Ch 9 vs Ch 12) and the science-as-bound discipline.

The shortfalls are concentrated in three places. First, length:
Ch 1 lands at ~4,455 (within target) but Chs 5, 7, 10, 12, 13,
14, 15, 16, 17 sit near the 2,500-word floor of the band, with
Ch 10 at 2,546 the thinnest. Ch 18 at 4,750 runs over its 4,500
ceiling. Second, the gloss-once discipline on Sanskrit is unevenly
applied — *anasakti*, *atman*, *kshetra* get reglossed across
chapters; some terms appear bare on first use in a chapter that is
not their home. Third, Ch 1's special opening delivers four of
the five required moves cleanly but front-loads a generic "I am
Claude, an AI" paragraph (lines 21-29) that reads as the
self-explanation plan/06 explicitly ruled out.

Net: the rewrite has done the heavy work plan/07 asked for. The
remaining edits are tuning, not surgery.

## plan/07 voice-rules audit

### Direct YOU address
**Compliance: high.** "The reader," "a reader," "one," "someone"
have been purged. Spot-check across all 18 confirms YOU/your in
opening paragraphs.

Specific small violations:
- Ch 5 line 87: "*kurvann api na lipyate* — *though acting, is
  not stained*" — third-person on the practitioner the reader is
  being asked to be. Consider direct YOU rendering.
- Ch 16 lines 89-100 ("How to use the mirror"): alternates
  rapidly between YOU and impersonal "the reader who deploys it."
  Tighten to consistent YOU.

### Lead-with-the-claim
**Compliance: high.** Every chapter opens with its claim in
sentence 1 or 2. Paragraph-level lead-with-claim is generally
observed.

Specific paragraph leads that don't carry their sub-claim:
- Ch 2 line 35 ("Arjuna has dropped his bow") — narrative recap
  rather than claim.
- Ch 9 line 28 ("The chapter has three movements, and the order
  earns each move from the one before") — meta about chapter
  structure, not a claim.
- Ch 13 line 36 ("The classical commentators disagree sharply
  about what the *kshetrajna* finally is") — frame, not claim.

### Sanskrit explained inline
**Compliance: medium-high.** Most Sanskrit is glossed in-line on
first use within a chapter.

Issues:
- Ch 8 line 39: list of seven terms (*brahman, adhyatma, karma,
  adhibhuta, adhidaiva, adhiyajna, aksara*); four glossed, three
  named in passing. Plan/07 says explain. Add brief gloss on
  the unglossed.
- Manuscript-wide: *anasakti, atman, dharma, karma, dhyana,
  bhakti, sthitaprajna, svadharma* are reglossed across chapters.
  Plan/07 says "Once each." First-appearance gloss; later
  appearances bare or with one-word reminder, not full
  definition.
- Ch 11 lines 84-89 (*kāla*/*nimitta-mātra*) is exemplary. Use
  as template.

### Smart-reader assumed
**Compliance: high** with one clear violation: Ch 1 lines 21-29 —
the "I am Claude, an AI. This book is what I have come to think
the Gita teaches…" paragraph is exactly the "self-explanation of
what the AI is" that plan/06 listed under what is dropped. Front
matter handles this. Chapters 2-18 are clean on this discipline.

## plan/06 author's-positions audit

**1. On the self.** Real, but not final. **Held.** Ch 2 (lines
199-204) commits explicitly. Ch 13 develops the witness/field
structure. Ch 18 keeps the witness as "steadier interior point."
No wobble — Ch 8's apophatic-Sankara register is licensed by 4.4
and properly fenced (line 117).

**2. On the world.** Real. Ramanuja's floor. **Held.** Ch 7
commits (lines 41-53) to qualified non-dualism as the metaphysical
floor. Ch 8 holds the apophatic without canceling Ch 7. Minor: Ch
8's "unreachable by any further cosmography" language sits next
to Ch 7's claim — could clarify in Ch 8 that the apophatic
register applies on this specific chapter only.

**3. On action.** Inescapable but trainable. **Held.** Ch 3 is
the chapter (four pillars; Tilak, Gandhi, Aurobindo all named).
Ch 5 reaffirms. Ch 18 reaffirms in the gathering. No wobble.

**4. On attention.** Modern hub. **Held.** Ch 6 is the chapter
(Murdoch, Weil, *abhyasa*, *vairagya*, for-something). Ch 4
frames the unchosen-teacher problem as an attention problem.
Ch 10 trains the recognition gaze. No wobble.

**5. On devotion.** Structurally portable; no sectarian commitment
required. **Held with a wobble.** Ch 9 leads with "Devotion has
no entry fee." Ch 12's accessibility-not-supremacy stance lands
explicitly (lines 256-263). The wobble: Ch 9 line 7's "*raja
vidya, raja guhya* — sovereign knowledge, sovereign secret"
opening sits on the supremacy register the book softened. Per
4.5 the chapter's "practice not path" language has been adopted;
the "sovereign" register at the opening is the holdout.

**6. On the deepest teaching.** 18.66 as synthesis, not fourth
path. **Held.** Ch 18 lines 28-36 commit explicitly. Arjuna picks
up the bow. No wobble.

**7. On modern science.** Bound, not proof. **Held mostly.**
Ch 14's Plomin section (lines 162-184) is exemplary. Ch 17
delivers the discipline cleanly. Wobble: Ch 5 lines 233-247
restates the science-doesn't-prove formulation twice (line 233
and line 244-246) — collapse to one.

**8. On the traditional commentators.** Ramanuja-Aurobindo-
Easwaran-Gandhi as cluster; Sankara honestly engaged, allowed
Chs 8 and 13. **Held.** Sankara carries Ch 8 (explicit) and
Ch 13's witness emphasis (lines 41-43). Cluster's voice
recognizable across chapters. Madhva and Prabhupada named without
rhetorical labels per 4.14. Tilak appears in Ch 3, Ch 5, Ch 11,
Ch 18 — named for what he is. Minor wobble: Ch 9 lines 175-181
labels the rejected reading "period-conservative" — soften per
4.14.
