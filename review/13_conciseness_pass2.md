# 13 — Conciseness Review (Post-Rewrite)

## Overall

The rewritten manuscript reads as a book, not a survey, and that is the major
gain. But the manuscript is not yet at its honest length. Across 88,500
words, the most consistent inflation is *the third example after the second
has already done the work*, *the metaphor extended one beat past where it
earned its keep*, and *the synthesis paragraph that restates the synthesis
just made one paragraph earlier*. The chapters have grown comfortable in the
new continuous-prose form; the discipline now needed is the trim that lets
the form breathe.

The bloat sits in three places. First, **enumeration overhang**: chapters
that name a structure (four pillars, three gates, five factors) often walk
the reader through every position with case-vignettes when one or two would
have settled it. Chapter 1's seven concrete situations and Chapter 18's five
Tuesday situations are the clearest cases. Second, **commentator-rotation
fatigue**: chapters routinely cycle Sankara/Ramanuja/Madhva/Aurobindo/
Gandhi/Prabhupada through the same disagreement when the chapter only needs
two of them to land its move. Third, **closure inflation**: many chapters
have closed once at the natural end of the argument and then closed again
with a coda paragraph or a final imperative.

A confident pass should aim for ~10,500–13,000 words trimmed (12–15%),
landing the book around 75,500–78,000 words. The cuts the manuscript needs
are surgical, not structural — no chapter wants to be re-architected; most
chapters want a paragraph removed here, a redundant example collapsed there,
a third metaphor pulled, a closing flourish let go.

## Manuscript-wide bloat patterns

Eight recurring patterns of inflation, in rough order of how much they
collectively cost.

**1. Three-or-more concrete-situation enumerations** when one or two would
do the argument's work. The rewrite has imported the survey-template's
"five concrete situations" structurally even when the section heading is
gone. Most chapters have one such list (Ch 1's seven, Ch 13's six, Ch 14's
six, Ch 18's five, Ch 15's four candidates, Ch 12's four-or-five
applications, Ch 9's five practical forms). These enumerations were
supposed to be replaced with continuous prose carrying one or two examples;
they have been retained as rhythmic features.

**2. Commentator-rotation fatigue.** Many chapters cycle through Sankara,
Ramanuja, Madhva, Aurobindo, Gandhi, Prabhupada in succession when the
chapter's argument only needs two or three voices to settle. The rewrite's
own discipline (cluster named in front matter, refer back) is being
under-used. Chs 5, 12, 13, 15, 16, 18 all run the full rotation when a
slimmer set would do.

**3. Closure inflation.** Many chapters close once at the natural end of
their argument, then close again with a coda paragraph or a final
imperative, then sometimes again with a "what you already know" or "what
survives" or "this is the chapter, in one sentence" beat. Chs 1, 4, 6, 10,
12, 14, 15, 16, 17 all carry double or triple closures.

**4. Restatement-as-conclusion at section end.** A subsection ends with a
paragraph that restates the move just made rather than concluding it.
Pattern: argument-paragraph, example-paragraph, restatement-paragraph,
section-break. The restatement-paragraph could go.

**5. Per-chapter restatement of the bound-not-proof science discipline.**
The validated intention specifically named this as a manuscript-wide cut
target. The rewrite has reduced but not eliminated the restatement; Chs 6,
8, 11, 12, 14, 17, 18 each contain at least one paragraph naming what the
science *does not* prove. Front matter is supposed to carry this; the
chapters can lose the formula.

**6. Over-qualification chains in modern-voice introductions.** "X argues
that Y, though Z, while acknowledging W, with the caveat that V" structure
appears across Chs 2, 4, 8, 11, 12, 13, 14, 17. The qualifications often
repeat work the surrounding paragraph has already done.

**7. Metaphor-extension past where it earns its keep.** A central image
gets introduced and worked, then re-worked one paragraph later in slightly
different terms. The lamp-in-windless-place (Ch 6), the inverted tree (Ch
15), the field/knower (Ch 13), the leaf (Ch 9), the kitchen (Ch 9) all
extend past their first earned use.

**8. AI-authorship and "I am declining" framing.** The validated intention
asked for the AI-authorship to be declared once, and for "I am keeping/I am
setting aside" cataloguing to be dropped. The rewrite has retained both in
softer form. Ch 1's closing AI disclosure and Ch 3's "what I am keeping
from each tradition" paragraph are the clearest carryovers.
