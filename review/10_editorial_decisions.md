# 10 — Editorial Decisions

The author's response to the four editorial reports (`06`, `07`, `08`,
`09`). This document commits to a set of changes, defers a set of changes
to a future phase, and binds the next pass of chapter refinement.

The four reports converge on roughly the same picture: the book holds
together, the through-line is legible, the §7 sections are the book's
strength, the §3 sections have become a tax on the reader, and a
front-matter piece is overdue. Each report adds specific moments and
specific recommendations on top of that shared diagnosis.

This document accepts the convergent findings, applies the specific
recommendations the author judges load-bearing, and defers content
additions (grief account, working-class vignettes, money chapter,
partnership chapter, parenting chapter, contemporary-spiritual
vocabulary) to a future phase.

---

## Part 1 — What is being applied now

### A. Front matter (created directly by author, this pass)

**A1. "What this book is for" piece** — `book/front_matter/01_what_this_book_is_for.md`. Names the through-line, the named audience, the modern-reader hub (attention), the metaphysical floor (Ch 7's qualified non-dualism), the governing disciplines ("bracketed metaphysics, kept practice"; "science as bound, not as proof"), and the manuscript's working interpretive cluster (Ramanuja–Aurobindo–Easwaran–Gandhi, with Sankara as carrying Chs 8 and 13).

**A2. Cast of commentators** — `book/front_matter/03_cast_of_commentators.md`. One short biographical-and-lens entry per recurring author (Sankara, Ramanuja, Madhva, Prabhupada, Tilak, Gandhi, Aurobindo, Easwaran, Mascaró, Miller, Schweig, Radhakrishnan, Patton, Edgerton, Johnson). Once this exists, chapters refer back to it rather than re-introducing each commentator's lens in every §3.

**A3. Title-and-attribution declaration** — `book/front_matter/00_title_and_attribution.md`. The plain-language AI-authorship declaration (already specified in plan/04).

**A4. Reading guide** — `book/front_matter/04_reading_guide.md`. Including the target reader's actual instruction: "if pressed for time, read Chs 1, 6, 8, 11, 13, 16, 17, 18 first; the §7 modern-application sections of every chapter are the book's center of gravity."

### B. Manuscript-wide cuts (applied via per-chapter refinement agents)

These apply in every chapter where they appear:

**B1. Cut the "what foregrounds / what backgrounds / what it commits to" tic in §3.** Replace each instance with one line of free prose. Use the formulation at most three times across the entire manuscript.

**B2. Cut "I am keeping / I am setting aside" cataloguing in §6.** Where two consecutive synthesis claims open with these phrases, rewrite the second to do its work without the formula. Convert any list-style "What I am keeping: X. What I am setting aside: Y." into continuous prose.

**B3. Cut pre-position throat-clearing.** Drop "I am going to take a position." "What follows is the position I am willing to defend." "Now I choose." "I have weighed the readings. Where I land:" Begin with the position itself.

**B4. Vary §1 openings — kill the recap-as-opener.** Specifically Chs 4, 5, 7, 9, 10, 12, 13, 14 currently open by recapping prior chapters. Apply the discipline of the strong openings (Chs 1, 11, 15, 16) — start *inside* the chapter.

**B5. Trim phone-as-exemplar.** Currently the smartphone appears as the central exemplar in roughly eight §7 sections. Limit to three chapters (Ch 6 — the structural-discipline chapter; Ch 8 — the threshold-shaping chapter; Ch 17 — the calendar/browser-history chapter). Other chapters substitute a different concrete (decision under uncertainty; grief; care work; the difficult conversation; the project that ended).

**B6. Cut "Take X" anaphora to maximum three iterations per §7.** Currently Chs 5, 13, 14, 18 use "Take" or its variants five-plus times in succession.

**B7. Drop count headers.** "Five concrete situations." "Six situations." "Seven scenarios." Drop the count; let the situations announce themselves. Apply across §7 sections.

**B8. Compress repeated commentator introductions.** Once the front-matter Cast of Commentators exists, in-chapter commentator introductions reduce to a name and lens-tag (e.g., "Sankara, reading through Advaita..."). Drop the standardized biography-bias paragraph that currently appears in every §3.

**B9. §5 science paragraph template.** Open subsections with the *finding*, not the citation. Treat the citation as parenthetical or footnote. Currently every §5 subsection opens "Researcher and colleagues at Institution published 'Title' in *Journal* Year" — kill this.

**B10. Bullet lists in §6 → sentences.** Where Chs 16 and 17 use bullet formats for "what I commit to / what I set aside," convert to prose paragraphs.

**B11. §0 epigraphs to one sentence.** Where epigraphs have grown to four or five sentences, trim back to the one or two-sentence shape that the strongest epigraphs (Chs 1, 6, 11, 15, 17) hold.

**B12. Anchor-verse multi-translator blocks** — pick one verse per chapter for the full multi-translator treatment. Other anchor verses get one or two translations, not seven. Currently Chs 1, 5, 8, 11, 14, 17, 18 stack three to seven translators on three or four verses each — the cumulative effect is encyclopedic rather than musical.

**B13. Audit weak closings.** Chs 3, 5, 6, 8, 10, 13, 14 — cut the last paragraph of thesis-restatement; let the second-to-last sentence carry. The strongest closings (Chs 1, 9, 11, 15, 17, 18) close on an image, an instruction, or a sentence that is not a thesis. Apply that standard.

**B14. Add prefatory pointer to manuscript-wide disciplines.** Where a §6 currently restates "bracketed metaphysics, kept practice" or "the science doesn't prove the chapter; it shows...", replace with one sentence pointing at front matter ("on the discipline of bracketing, see front matter"). Removed restatements: Chs 2, 4, 8, 10, 12, 17.

### C. Per-chapter specific changes

**C1. Ch 1.** Verify the §6 affective caricature of Sankara was actually removed in the conciseness pass. The skeptic surfaces Ch 1 §6's "compassion that paralyzes is not yet the operative compassion of someone who knows what to do" attributed to Sankara — Sankara would not say "not yet"; that's developmental framing foreign to him. Reframe as Claude's reading rather than as Sankara's.

**C2. Ch 2 §6.** The conciseness pass softened "regulative not falsifiable" but left a second paragraph ("I am not claiming the *atman* exists in the way Sankara or Ramanuja claim. I am claiming the chapter's use of the imperishable...") that pulls the chapter back into the older minimalism. Cut or rewrite that paragraph so Ch 2 sits cleanly underneath Ch 7's metaphysical floor. The chapter's *practical* claim (agency without grafting) holds; the metaphysical hedge in the second paragraph contradicts the front matter.

**C3. Ch 5 §6.** The Sankara-graft move ("borrows Sankara's diagnosis while declining his prescription") needs an honesty upgrade. Add one sentence acknowledging that the strength being borrowed is one Sankara's framework specifically supplies, and that the naturalist reframe is *Claude's* move, not a synthesis Sankara would recognize. Skeptic 1, prose stylist drift moment.

**C4. Ch 6 §6.** Thicken the "for-something" criterion. "Larger than the small self" is what a Headspace ad would accept. The chapter rejects secular-mindfulness flattening but currently does so on a criterion that does not actually exclude the popular form. Add a sharper criterion — perhaps: *the horizon must be a real other (a person, a craft, a divine, a task one has undertaken on behalf of something one did not author) rather than the practitioner's own optimization*. Skeptic 5.

**C5. Ch 7 §6.** Soften the "creed" register. Ch 7 is the manuscript's metaphysical-confessional moment, but the §6 currently reads in a register the rest of the book does not occupy. Trim the metaphysical-cosmological prose that pulls Ch 7 into a different voice. Cohesiveness flag #2.

**C6. Ch 9 §6.** The 9.32 reading (Gandhi's anti-caste reading vs. period-conservative concession reading) is currently presented as the chapter's reading without acknowledging that we are choosing the politically congenial reading over the historically careful one. Add one sentence flagging that this is a choice, with reasoning. Skeptic 6.

**C7. Ch 9 vs Ch 12 — bhakti register.** Ch 9 still reads bhakti as the chapter's *answer* with sectarian-universalist heat (per cohesiveness #1). Align Ch 9 §6's stance with Ch 12 §6: accessibility, not supremacy. The conciseness pass made the wording change ("path the chapter is naming" → "practice the chapter is opening"); some §6 register issues remain.

**C8. Ch 10 §3.** Rewrite as one sustained comparative paragraph treating six readings together with one quoted hook per reading, rather than six structurally identical subsections. The chapter is supposed to train recognition through accumulation; the §3 currently catalogues rather than accumulates. Prose stylist top recommendation #10.

**C9. Ch 11 §7.** The climate application slips from §6's careful "you are *nimitta-matra*, not *kala*" into §7's "you are inside *kala* at planetary scale" — a register change the §6 just chastised. Bring §7's climate prose into alignment with §6's epistemic shape. Skeptic 3, cohesiveness flag #4.

**C10. Ch 14 §6.** The "I am keeping / I am setting aside" cycle is the chapter's most rhythm-killing pattern (prose stylist top recommendation #9). Rewrite §6 as continuous prose with each move embedded in a paragraph. Drop "I am keeping" / "I am setting aside" framing entirely. AND: cut "I am not asking the reader to commit to Brahman" (cohesiveness flag #5) — that's the regulative-only minimalism Ch 7 has displaced.

**C11. Ch 14 §5.1.** Plomin's heritability data does not license the "training is unbounded" gloss. Add a sentence honoring what Plomin actually shows (substantial trait variance is *not* trainable) before pivoting to the training claim. Skeptic 10.

**C12. Ch 16 §6 and §7.** The fourth-gate (Arendt's thoughtlessness) extension is now flagged as Claude's modern-self-diagnostic addition (per validated 4.9). But §7 then deploys it as if it were on equal footing with the chapter's three. Constrain §7 to apply the fourth gate as Claude's recommended use, not as the chapter's typology. Skeptic 8.

**C13. Ch 17 §5.2.** The Rozin finding underwrites that food matters morally; it does not underwrite the *guna*-taxonomy of foods specifically. Bound the claim accordingly. Skeptic 9.

**C14. Ch 18 §3 / §6.** The 18.66 multi-translator block can stay as the manuscript's pinnacle anchor-verse moment (this is one of the cases B12 keeps). The "synthesis, not a fourth path" framing in §6 has landed; preserve.

### D. Voice-and-rhythm cleanups (applied via chapter agents)

These are prose-level touch-ups the prose stylist surfaced and the chapter agents will execute alongside the per-chapter changes:

- **D1.** Where chapters open with a participial wind-up ("Arjuna, having heard...", "By the end of Chapter 9..."), trim to a direct opener.
- **D2.** Cut self-deprecating throat clears ("This is harder than it sounds." "Almost embarrassingly direct." "Not by accident.").
- **D3.** Convert "On its face, X. In substance, Y." formula (Chs 14, 16) to a single direct claim.
- **D4.** Where italicized definitional self-glossing appears ("By 'non-reductive personalist' I mean..."), let the gloss be the next sentence's claim.
- **D5.** Cross-chapter catalogs inserted into the body ("Each will reappear: ...") — move to back-matter Threads index, not body prose.
- **D6.** Closing benedictions with "begin where you stand" / "begin where you are" repetition — vary or cut.

## Part 2 — What is being deferred

These are real findings the author accepts as accurate but will not address in this pass. They become future-phase work.

### E1. Missing content (target reader's "What's missing")

- Working account of slow-decline grief — for the parent-in-decline / partner-in-illness reader. Will require a substantial section, possibly its own threaded sub-section across Chs 1, 2, 6, 8, 12.
- Working-class examples in §7 sections — the rideshare driver, the warehouse worker, the nurse on a 12-hour shift. The book's stated audience is wider than its examples.
- Money — *anasakti* applied to a 401(k), *lobha* applied to housing-cost anxiety, *yajna* applied to giving. Currently treated obliquely.
- Romantic partnership — choosing, ending, sustaining a partnership. Chapter 12 gestures; the manuscript does not give a sustained meditation.
- Parenting / children — mentioned but not addressed.
- Integrated practice scheme — the book asks for half-a-dozen disciplines and does not give the reader a map of how they fit.
- Engagement with contemporary spiritual-but-not-religious vocabulary — IFS, men's-work / women's-work, contemplative-tech, psychedelic-renaissance.

These are accepted as gaps. They are not addressed in this pass.

### E2. Citation cleanup at scale

The skeptic correctly flags that "edition unconfirmed; validation pending" appears at scale across the manuscript. Resolving this requires access to the actual published editions of every cited work — Sankara, Ramanuja, Madhva, Aurobindo, Gandhi, Tilak, Prabhupada, Easwaran, Mascaró, Miller, Schweig, Radhakrishnan and the modern voices. This is genuine validation work, not editorial revision, and it is deferred to a separate validation phase.

### E3. The "cluster doing too much work" structural critique

The skeptic correctly observes that the Ramanuja–Aurobindo–Easwaran–Gandhi cluster is doing triangulation work that is really one interpretive family in four voices. The fix would require either (a) genuinely diversifying the synthesis cluster to include voices outside this family, or (b) explicitly naming in front matter that the manuscript reads through this cluster and that triangulation among them is not external corroboration. **Applying (b) here.** (a) is deferred.

### E4. Genuine alternate readings of disputed verses

The skeptic notes places where the manuscript chose the politically congenial reading and named the historically careful one "conservative" (e.g., Ch 9 §6 on 9.32). This pass adds a flag (per C6); a fuller treatment that gives the period-historical reading more substantive engagement is deferred.
