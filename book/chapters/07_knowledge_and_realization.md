# Chapter 7 — The Source

Hear now, Arjuna, what knowledge is when it is finally lived
rather than rehearsed. I will tell you what I am, and what stands
on me, and how few come to see it as it is.

Most who hear what I am about to say will hold it as a
proposition — turn it over, agree with it, file it. That is not
what I mean by knowing. There is knowing, and there is
realization. The first is the map. The second is the country. You
can carry an accurate map your whole life and never set foot.

What I am giving you now is the country.

Among many thousands who set out, a few make their way through.
Among the few who make their way, fewer still see me as I am. It
is not a gate I am keeping shut. It is a depth that takes time.
Most stop at the first thing that satisfies the wish to have
arrived. You, Arjuna, have not been allowed that. You sit on a
chariot you cannot drive away from. This is a kind of grace.

Listen.

There are two natures, and you are made of both.

The first is what you can name and weigh. Earth, water, fire, air,
the open space they move in. And the inner instruments that
process all of it — the thinking that thinks, the discriminating
that decides, the sense of a self at the center saying *I.* These
eight together I call my lower nature. Not lower as a slur. Lower
as the floor is lower than the room. It is what the world is built
of. Your body is here. Your moods are here. The mind that has been
turning since before dawn is here. None of this is illusion. None
of it is unworthy. It is the ground of every actual day.

But there is a second nature, higher, and harder to point at,
because it is doing the pointing. It is the conscious life that
holds all of the above — that is awake to the body, awake to the
thinking, awake to the I-sense itself. Without this, none of the
first nature would appear at all. Earth would not be earth to
anyone. Grief would not be grief to anyone. The lower nature is
what is held. The higher nature is the holding.

Both are mine. The world is held in being from within, by what I
am, the way a thread holds a string of beads — invisible, central,
the reason the line does not scatter. *On me all this is strung.*

Take this in for a moment, because it is the whole teaching.

I am the taste in water. Not the water — the taste. The thing in
it that meets you when you drink. I am the light in moon and sun.
Not the disc — the radiance, the actual giving of light that makes
seeing possible. I am the sound that carries in space. The
fragrance in the earth after rain. The brilliance in fire that
makes it fire and not merely heat. I am life itself in everything
that lives — the persistent reaching toward more life. The seed in
every being, that around which the being grows.

I am the intelligence in those who think clearly. The strength in
those who are strong. The courage in those who hold their ground.
The desire in the desiring, when desire is not at war with what is
right.

When you meet any real and good quality in any creature, you are
meeting me. When water tastes like water, that is me. When your
friend is brave on a hard day, that is me. The meal that
nourishes, the page that comes clear, the warm hand of the one you
love. I am intimate with the world. I am not behind it.

Now, the modes. You will have heard of them: the clear, the
restless, the heavy. Clarity that lifts the mind toward what is
real. Restlessness that drives the mind toward what it does not
have. Dullness that weighs the mind toward inertia and the easy
mistake. These three thread through every creature, every hour,
every act. They come from me. Everything you call clear, restless,
or dull rises from my nature.

But hear this, Arjuna, because here most go astray: I come from
them. I am not in them. They are in me. The way weather is in the
sky. The sky is not the weather. The sky is what the weather
happens in. I am the field where clarity, restlessness, and
dullness arise and pass. I am not bound by any of the three.

This is why so few see me as I am. The modes are vivid. They press
on the senses, color the mind, organize the day. People take what
the modes are doing for what is finally real. They identify with a
mood, a temperament, a season of restlessness or sleep, and miss
the one in whom moods occur. They take the appearance for the
source.

Even so, four kinds of people turn toward what is highest, and all
four are dear to me.

The one in distress, emptied of options, who finally turns. The
one who seeks knowledge, who wants to understand. The one who
seeks gain — yes, even this one, who comes asking for relief or
success and stays long enough to be changed by the asking. And the
one who already knows, who has tasted what I am and turns out of
love.

All four are dear. Do not despise the first three. Distress that
turns toward me is already half-way home. The seeker of knowledge
will not stop until knowledge becomes realization. The seeker of
gain, if the turn is real, finds a gain stranger and quieter than
the one first sought.

But the one with knowledge — for whom seeing me has become the
central fact, in whom there is no distance left between what they
know and what they are — that one is closest. That one I count as
my own self. Through many lives such a one is shaped, and at the
end says simply: *all this is the source.* Such people are rare.

You are at the beginning of that road, Arjuna. Do not be deceived
by the appearance. Do not take the modes for the field. Hold this
in mind as we go on, because what follows depends on it.
