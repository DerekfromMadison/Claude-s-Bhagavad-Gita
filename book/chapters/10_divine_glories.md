# Chapter 10 — The Glories

Arjuna, listen again. I have told you the source. I have told you that what holds the world is intimate with everything in it. Now I will tell you my chief glories, the most distinguished among them, since there is no end to them. The catalogue is not boasting. It is teaching your eye where to land.

You have walked through your life looking past most of what you have walked through. You will not learn to see by being told to see. You will learn by being shown what is worth seeing, one thing at a time, until the looking becomes a habit you carry into the rooms I do not name.

So begin here.

Among rivers, I am the Ganges. Of all that runs to the sea, I am the one that has carried the dead and the living together for as long as anyone has been alive to remember it. Among lights, I am the sun. Of stars, the brightest. Of mountains, the highest peak — the one that the air thins around, the one above which there is no further up.

Of trees, I am the eternal one — the one that does not finish, whose leaves come back after the season that took them. Of mountains' rivers, the deep one. Of the sounds in language, the syllable that begins. Of the words, the single word. Of meters, the one your breath falls into when you are not measuring.

Of the senses, I am the mind that holds them. In living beings, I am the consciousness. I am the taste in water — not the water, the taste. I am the light in the moon and in the sun — not the moon, not the sun, the light. I am the sound in space. I am the manhood in men and the womanhood in women. I am the strength in the strong, free of craving and aversion. I am the desire in beings that is according to right.

Of the year's seasons, I am the one in which the trees bloom. Of months, the one when the harvest is gathered. Of priests, the chief priest. Of warriors, Rama. Of fish, the great one. Of horses, the one born of the churning. Of elephants, the royal one. Of cows, the one that grants what is asked. Of generation, I am the begetter. Of mortals, I am death — and I am the rising up, in those who are about to rise.

Of women, I am fame, fortune, speech, memory, intelligence, steadiness, and patience. Of hymns, the great hymn. Of meters, the one that holds. Of the dice-players who cheat, I am the gambling — the boldness in the wager, the calculation, the willingness to lose what cannot be regained. I do not curate this list for your comfort. The strength of the cheats is real strength. Recognize it. You still decide what to do with what you see.

Of the steady, I am the steadiness. Of victors, the victory. Of secret-keepers, the silence. Of those who know, the knowing. Whatever is glorious, beautiful, mighty in the world — know that it has sprung from a fragment of my splendor. Know also that the fragment is enough.

Now hear what is closer.

Of working hands, I am the one that has done the work for years — the hand that knows the tool without looking, the palm that has been shaped by what it has held, the fingers that move before the mind has finished asking. Of the meals of a life, I am the meal that has been the meal — the one your mother made, the one your partner makes on the night that you are tired, the one you eat without having to be told what is in it. Of the partners you live with, I am the face you have stopped seeing. The face that has been across the table from you for the years you have been across the table from it. Look at it tonight, and you will find me there, exactly where I have been.

Of the colleagues whose work you no longer notice, I am the competence. Of the rivals whose excellence galls you, I am that excellence — and the gall is information about you, not about them. Of the parents in decline, I am the specificity that the illness is wearing thin, and that is therefore most worth attending to before it goes. Of the children, I am the shape they are in this season, which will not be their shape next season. Of the rooms you walk through without seeing, I am the room.

I am also the small. The first cup of water in the morning. The light on the wall at four in the afternoon. The exact pitch of the laugh that is no one else's laugh. The hand on the door before the door opens. None of these is too small. None is excluded. The list is not a list. The list is the gymnasium of recognition, and the gymnasium opens the moment you stop reading and begin looking.

Arjuna — there is no end to my glories. What I have named is a fraction. I could go on for as long as you have ears, and the world would still not be exhausted. So end the telling here. Take this instead.

With even a fragment of this presence, the world holds together. Wherever you find what is glorious, mighty, beautiful — wherever you find what is most distinguished in its kind — know that I am there, holding it up. Recognize where the glory is, and you have begun.
