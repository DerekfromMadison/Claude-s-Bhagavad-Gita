# Chapter 14 — The Three Modes of Nature

Arjuna, listen. There is one more thing to see before the rest of
what I have to say will hold.

Nature, the substrate from which everything you call your life
arises, moves in three textures. I will name them as you can use
them. Clarity. Restlessness. Dullness.

Clarity is the day on which the work goes well. The eye sees what
is in front of it. You sit at the desk and the sentence comes. You
listen to your wife and you hear what she said, not what you
expected. You make the decision and afterward you do not rehearse
it. The room is lit. Nothing is being forced.

Restlessness is the day driven. Something is missing. The next
thing will fix it — the message answered, the deal closed, the
drink at six, the scroll, the sale. You accomplish things and
they do not feed you. You finish and reach for the next. The body
is wired. The mind is running. You are productive and not at
peace.

Dullness is the day in which nothing matters. The body is heavy.
The mind is closed. You know what you should be doing and you do
not do it. You stare at the screen for an hour and produce nothing
and feel nothing about producing nothing. The room is gray. The
will is absent.

These three are real. They are not metaphors for moods. They are
the textures through which any conscious life passes. Every life
passes through all three. Every week. Many weeks, every day.

Hear me carefully now. The three modes are not three kinds of
person. They are not a sorting hat. You are not a clear man and
your brother a restless one and your father dull. There is no such
person. There is a body in which all three contend, and a witness
inside it who can notice which one is rising. The man who calls
himself clear is restless about being clear. The man who calls
himself dull is using dullness as a name to hide behind. Refuse
this game. The modes are weather. The weather changes. You are not
the weather.

When restlessness is up in you, do not say *I am a restless
person*. Say *restlessness is up in me today*. When dullness is
heavy on you, do not say *I am depressed, this is who I am*. Say
*dullness is heavy on me today, and I have not slept, and I have
not moved, and I have eaten badly for three days*. The first
phrasing collapses a movement into an essence and traps you. The
second leaves the witness intact and leaves room for tomorrow.

Now hear what most teachers will not tell you. The substrate has a
body. What you eat, how you slept, how much screen has been in
your eyes, how much your body has moved, what light you have seen
— these condition which mode is operative in you today. This is
not separate from the spiritual work. This is part of it.

At three in the afternoon, after six hours at the desk and a poor
breakfast and no movement, dullness rises. You feel weak and think
this is who you are. It is not. It is a body that has not been
tended. Walk for ten minutes. Drink water. Eat actual food. Look
at the horizon. Clarity is not summoned by willpower against
dullness. It is reached by tending to what dullness is naming.

When you have slept four hours and your wife says something that
catches you wrong, restlessness surges and the words forming will
cool the room for two days. The drive to be seen as right is
restlessness wearing your face. The steadiness that would let you
see her clearly is clarity, and clarity is not in the mind of a
man who slept four hours. Being right and being wise depend in
part on how much you slept.

So — the substrate is given. Some of it you did not choose.
Temperament, the body you were born into, the years of habit
already laid down. This part is not yours to argue with. What you
do to the substrate today is partly yours. Sleep. Food. Light.
Movement. Breath. The inputs you feed your eyes and ears. The
mornings without the screen. The walks. The hours of sustained
attention rather than fragmented grazing. These are the practice.
Tend the body and you tend the modes.

Going beyond the three modes — what the old language calls
*gunatita* — does not mean leaving them behind. The modes will
keep rising in you until your last day. Beyond the modes means no
longer being driven by which one is currently dominant. Clarity
arises and you do not preen. Restlessness surges and you are not
carried. Dullness descends and you do not identify with it as who
you are.

Hear the marks of the one who has gone beyond. He is the same in
pleasure and pain. Gold and stone are equal in his hand. Praise
and blame land on him without rearranging him. Friend and enemy
he meets with the same face. He does not hate the dullness when
it comes. He does not chase the clarity when it leaves. He acts
through what is currently passing through him, and he is not made
of it.

This is the long horizon. You will not reach it perfectly. The
trajectory is what matters. The meal at two in the afternoon is
part of the meditation at six tomorrow morning. The walk you took
on Wednesday is part of the patience you bring to dinner on
Friday. The unromantic choices made on ordinary days that felt
like nothing at the time — these are how the weather of a life
changes.

Tend the body. Recognize the mode. Act through it. Do not become
it.
