# Chapter 13 — The Field and Its Knower

Krishna:

Arjuna, hear this carefully. This body is called the field. The one who knows it is called the knower of the field.

Take that as the seam to learn first, before any other teaching of mine settles into your life.

The field is what is given to you to work with. The body — bones, breath, the heat in the chest, the ache behind the eye. The senses — what they reach toward, what they recoil from. The mind, with its running commentary and its weather. The I-sense, which gathers it all under one name and calls itself you. Desire, aversion, pleasure, pain, the felt continuity of being a particular person with a particular history. Patience and impatience, fortitude and its failures. All of this — listen — is the field. All of it is what is observed.

The knower of the field is the consciousness in which all of that appears. Not itself in the field. Not catchable as a thing among things. The light by which the field is seen, not a brighter object inside the field.

Arjuna, you have lived your whole life on the field's side of the seam. When you say I, you mean the body, the history, the role. When the body is in pain, you say I am in pain, and the saying closes a loop that has no air in it. When the mind catastrophizes at four in the morning, you say I am catastrophizing, and there is no aperture. When the email stings, you become the sting. When the headache lasts three days, you become the headache.

Hear me. The pain is real. The body is having it. You are having it. I am not telling you to pretend otherwise. I am telling you that the noticing is happening alongside the pain, in something that is not itself wounded by what it notices. There is a small operational difference between *I am pain* and *there is pain in this body, and I am the one noticing it*. The first is closed. The second has an opening. The opening is where patience gets its grip.

Arjuna:

Krishna, when I try to find this knower, I find only more field. Thoughts about the knower. Images of the knower. None of them are it.

Krishna:

That is correct, and that is the whole point. The moment you turn to look at the knower, what you have caught is field. The knower is the looking, not what is looked at. Try to catch your own seeing in the act of seeing and you find an image of seeing, a thought about seeing, which is itself something seen. The knower recedes as you reach for it. Do not be discouraged by this. The recession is the proof that you are pointing in the right direction.

You ask how then to know it. Not by grasping. By living in such a way that the seam becomes obvious in use.

Hear the qualities of one who knows. Humility — the ground from which the seam is even visible. Honesty — no pretending. Non-violence — toward the body that is your field, toward the bodies that are the fields of others. Patience — long, slow patience with what cannot be hurried. Simplicity. Devotion to a teacher who has walked this. Cleanness, inner and outer. Steadiness in your seat. Restraint of the senses when they would drag the field around at their pleasure. The clear-eyed perception that birth, old age, disease, and death are real conditions of every field. Non-clinging to wife, son, house, the small kingdom you have built around yourself. An evenness through what arrives and what leaves. Devotion to me, undivided. Solitude, sometimes, for the sake of the seeing. The willingness to dwell with what cannot be made small.

These are not decorations. Without them, the talk of field and knower is air. With them, the distinction stops being a doctrine and becomes the way you breathe.

Arjuna, listen now to the harder thing. The same knower is in every body. Not a copy in each, not a fragment apportioned per person. The same. The light by which you see your field is the light by which your enemy sees his. This is not a softening of the war. It is a clarification of its scale. You are about to act in a field, and the action is yours. But do not mistake the field for everything that is.

Take it now into your week. The headache: the body is in pain, the noticing is not in pain. Name where the field is. Name where the knower is. The anxious afternoon: the mind is racing, the watching is not racing. The email that stung: the sting is in the field, the awareness of the sting is not stung. The work that has eaten you for years: the work is field. You are the knower. Its outcomes matter. They do not constitute you.

The grief, when it comes — and it will come, for someone you love is going to die, and someone has already — is field. Real, total-feeling, complete in its weight. The one who grieves is not identical with the grief. Honor the loss. Do not pretend the grief away. Continue, slowly, to act from the steadier interior.

This is wisdom, Arjuna. Not the recitation of the distinction. The lived practice of telling the difference. Sit. Watch the field. Notice that you are not the field. Stand up and act, from the place that was watching.

The bow is still in front of you. The field is the field. You are not.
