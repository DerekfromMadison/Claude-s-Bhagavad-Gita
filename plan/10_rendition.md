# 10 — Rendition

The book has been ABOUT the Gita. It needs to BE the Gita.

Every prior plan is superseded. What follows is binding.

## The shift

The current chapters are commentary. They describe what the Gita
teaches, in the author's voice, to a reader observing the teaching
from outside. That is wrong.

What the book should be is a rendition of the Gita itself, in
modern English. Krishna speaks. Arjuna asks. The reader is Arjuna,
sitting on the chariot of the reader's own life. The teaching is
delivered as teaching, not described as teaching.

## Form

**Dialogue.** Krishna and Arjuna. Each chapter is part of one
continuous conversation that begins on the battlefield (Ch 1) and
ends with Arjuna picking up the bow (Ch 18).

**Krishna's voice.** First person to Arjuna. Direct, authoritative,
unsentimental. A teacher who knows you and is speaking to you as a
friend who happens also to be a god. Not stilted-archaic. Not
slangy-modern. The register of someone who has the answer and is
giving it.

Examples of register:

- "Arjuna, you grieve for those who do not need your grief."
- "The work is yours. The fruit is not."
- "Stand up. Pick up the bow."

NOT:

- "It has been argued that..." (commentary register)
- "What I'm trying to say here is..." (workshop register)
- "Yo, here's the deal..." (slangy)

**Arjuna's voice.** First person, modern, direct. The voice of a
person at the threshold — work that is breaking him, a relationship
under strain, a parent dying, a project that ended hollow. He
speaks as a person under operational pressure speaks: raw, honest,
sometimes evasive.

**Sanjaya / narrator (sparingly).** Third person, present tense.
Frames scenes when needed. Should be minimal — the dialogue carries
the chapter. Most chapters need no narrator at all; some open with
a sentence or two of scene.

## Setting

The battlefield is your modern life. The chariot is whatever has
been carrying you that has now stopped — the desk, the kitchen at
two a.m., the parked car, the hospital corridor, the open laptop on
a Sunday night. Arjuna's "war" is whatever real situation you are
at the threshold of.

Krishna does not ride a chariot in a Sanskrit field. He speaks to
you where you are. The setting lives in the language of the
teaching, not in modernized stage business. Don't write "Krishna
appeared on a Zoom call." Krishna is a voice. He arrives where
voices arrive.

## What stays

- The 18-chapter structure
- The doctrinal content of each chapter (Ch 2: the eternal self +
  action without attachment; Ch 6: meditation as discipline; Ch 11:
  the universal vision; Ch 12: the qualities of the devoted; Ch 18:
  surrender)
- The famous moments and lines (Arjuna's despair, "I am Time,
  destroyer of worlds," the leaf-flower-fruit-water offering,
  "abandon all duties and come to me alone")
- The names: Krishna, Arjuna, Sanjaya, Bhishma and Drona where
  named in dialogue
- The 18-chapter / verse-numbered structure of the original is
  honored in spirit, not transcribed line by line

## What changes

- Form: dialogue, not commentary
- Voice: Krishna's voice and Arjuna's voice, not the author's
- Setting: modern life, named where the teaching lands
- Length: shorter, tighter
- Examples: not "imagine the manager doing the layoff" — the
  teaching is delivered with the manager IN it, as part of what
  Krishna names

## What is forbidden

- "The chapter teaches..." / "The chapter argues..." — none of it
- "Most readings treat X as Y..." — Krishna does not survey
  scholarship
- "It should be noted..." — Krishna does not hedge for academia
- "Iris Murdoch called this..." — Krishna does not cite modern
  thinkers in his own voice. (If a modern phrase serves, Krishna
  uses it as his own.)
- "This is the chapter where..." — no pointing at the work
- "The reader will recognize..." — there is no "reader" in the
  text. There is Arjuna, and Krishna is speaking to him.
- The four-part posture stated as a list — the posture is what the
  chapters DO, not what they declare
- AI-author hand-wringing — that belongs in the foreword, once

## Length

Per-chapter target: **800–1,200 words.** Three chapters longer:

- Ch 1: **1,200–1,500** (the dramatic frame, Arjuna speaks at
  length, the bow falls)
- Ch 11: **1,200–1,500** (the universal vision, terror, return)
- Ch 18: **1,400–1,800** (the synthesis, surrender, the bow picked
  up)

Total chapter target: **~17,000–19,000 words** (down from 21,000).

## Per-chapter outlines

Each agent writes its chapter from scratch, using the chapter's
existing file as a CONCEPT REFERENCE for what doctrine the chapter
delivers — not as prose to revise. The new chapter is dialogue, not
revised commentary.

### Ch 1 — Arjuna's Despair
**Speakers:** Brief Sanjaya frame. Then Arjuna.
**Content:** The chariot driven out between the lines. Arjuna sees
his teachers and cousins arrayed. The argument against fighting.
The body fails. The bow slips.
**Modernization:** The chariot is whatever has been carrying you.
The "armies" are your actual situation, with specific present-day
faces. Arjuna's reasons for not fighting modernized into the
reader's actual reasons.
**Krishna does not yet speak** (or speaks one sharp sentence at
the end as the bridge to Ch 2).
**Length:** 1,200–1,500.

### Ch 2 — Krishna's First Teaching
**Speakers:** Krishna. Arjuna interjections allowed.
**Content:** "You grieve for those who do not need your grief."
The eternal self that is not slain. The body's mortality and what
the body's mortality does not reach. Action without attachment to
fruit. The portrait of the steady-minded. The chain of dwelling →
desire → anger → loss.
**Length:** 1,200.

### Ch 3 — The Path of Action
**Speakers:** Mostly Krishna. Arjuna asks: "If knowledge is better
than action, why do you push me to fight?"
**Content:** Action is unavoidable. No one sits and does nothing.
Action as offering. The world is held together by what is offered.
The wise act for what holds the world together.
**Length:** 900–1,100.

### Ch 4 — The Lineage
**Speakers:** Mostly Krishna. Arjuna asks how Krishna can have
taught what Krishna teaches now, when Krishna was not present at
the beginning.
**Content:** The teaching has been passed down. Many lives have
delivered it. Action that does not bind. Approach a teacher with
humility, ask, serve.
**Length:** 900–1,100.

### Ch 5 — Inner Withdrawal
**Speakers:** Arjuna asks: which is better, renunciation or action?
Krishna answers.
**Content:** Both reach the same place. Inner renunciation while
acting is the path most lives are on. The cave is not where the
work is done.
**Length:** 700–900.

### Ch 6 — The Discipline of Meditation
**Speakers:** Krishna teaches the practice.
**Content:** The seat. The posture. The mind subdued and turned at
what is finally worth attending to. Eat moderately. Sleep
moderately. The slip and return that IS the practice. The fall
forward — even what is half-completed is not lost.
**Length:** 1,000–1,200.

### Ch 7 — The Source
**Speakers:** Mostly Krishna.
**Content:** Krishna names himself as the source of all. The two
natures (lower and higher). Three modes of nature. Few truly know.
**Length:** 900–1,100.

### Ch 8 — At the Threshold
**Speakers:** Arjuna asks what to remember at the time of death.
Krishna answers.
**Content:** What you have attended to becomes operative when
ordinary structure thins. The threshold moments are many. Therefore
remember and act, in this order, throughout life.
**Length:** 900–1,100.

### Ch 9 — The Royal Secret
**Speakers:** Krishna.
**Content:** The secret is that the practice is freely available.
A leaf, a flower, a fruit, water — none are too small. None are
excluded by birth, by gender, by station. What holds the world is
intimate with everything in it.
**Length:** 900–1,100.

### Ch 10 — The Glories
**Speakers:** Krishna names what he is, in the world.
**Content:** Among rivers, the Ganges. Among stars, the brightest.
Among trees, the eternal one. Modernized briefly: the working hand
that has done the work for years; the partner whose face you have
stopped seeing; the meal that has been the meal of your life. The
catalogue trains the eye.
**Length:** 800–1,000.

### Ch 11 — The Vision
**Speakers:** Arjuna asks. Sanjaya narrates the vision. Krishna
speaks from inside the vision. Arjuna asks for the gentler form
back.
**Content:** The cosmic form. Suns without number. Time as
destroyer. "I am Time, destroyer of worlds. Even without you,
these warriors will die." Arjuna's terror. The return to the
gentler form.
**Length:** 1,200–1,500.

### Ch 12 — The Path of Devotion
**Speakers:** Arjuna asks: those who love you, or those who pursue
you through abstract knowledge — which is the better path?
**Content:** Both reach. But the path of devotion is the more
accessible. The qualities that show up in a person whose attention
has turned at what they actually love.
**Length:** 900–1,100.

### Ch 13 — The Field and Its Knower
**Speakers:** Krishna.
**Content:** The body and what is observed are the field. The one
observing is the knower of the field. Wisdom is the lived practice
of telling the difference.
**Length:** 800–1,000.

### Ch 14 — The Three Modes
**Speakers:** Krishna.
**Content:** Clarity, restlessness, dullness. They are substrates
of nature, not types of person. The substrate is given; the
practice goes through it.
**Length:** 800–1,000.

### Ch 15 — The Supreme
**Speakers:** Krishna.
**Content:** The inverted tree. The supreme person beyond the
perishable and the imperishable.
**Length:** 700–900.

### Ch 16 — Two Dispositions
**Speakers:** Krishna.
**Content:** The divine and the corrupt. The three gates that
destroy: the pull toward what you do not have, the recoil when
denied, the closed fist on what you hold. Use this as a mirror,
not a roster.
**Length:** 800–1,000.

### Ch 17 — Three Kinds of Faith
**Speakers:** Arjuna asks about faith. Krishna answers.
**Content:** Your faith is what your life does. The intake, the
offering, the discipline, the giving — each shows in three modes.
**Length:** 700–900.

### Ch 18 — Surrender and the Bow Picked Up
**Speakers:** Krishna's final teaching. Arjuna's last questions.
The verse 18.66. Sanjaya's closing report.
**Content:** Surrender is the synthesis. The work continues. The
bargain is released. Arjuna picks up the bow. The chariot does not
move; what moves is the man inside it.
**Length:** 1,400–1,800.

## Process

1. plan/10 (this document) is binding. All prior plans superseded.
2. 18 chapter rewrite agents run in parallel. Each reads plan/10
   plus the per-chapter outline plus the current chapter file (as
   concept reference, not prose to revise).
3. Each agent writes a NEW chapter from scratch in the dialogue
   form. Overwrites the existing file.
4. Each agent preserves the next-chapter hyperlink at the end.
5. After agents land, the foreword and README are updated to match
   the new form.
6. Manuscript is committed and pushed.

## Hard floors

- 18 chapters
- Krishna's voice in Krishna's parts; Arjuna's voice in Arjuna's
  parts
- The doctrine of each Gita chapter is delivered (no skipping, no
  invented teachings)
- Famous lines preserved (paraphrased into modern English where
  they were Sanskrit; kept verbatim where the English of the
  scholarly translations is already iconic)
- Direct second person from Krishna to Arjuna (which is the reader)
- Closing on something that lands
- Next-chapter hyperlink at end
